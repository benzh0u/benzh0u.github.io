Fading Memories

April 2022

Her wrinkled hands grip
the wooden spoon,
stirring memories
in a weathered black wok.

Walls of dark green
cradle her,
as she hums a tune
half forgotten.

Her clouded eyes search
for faces she once knew,
names like autumn leaves
vanishing at first frost.

As she leaves the wok,
her wooden spoon keeps stirring,
tracing circles in the frail air,
each spin a name
a laugher
a face forgotten.
