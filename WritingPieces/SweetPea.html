Sweet Pea

April 2022

Her wrinkling stem droops
as I pass by
the garden's edge.

Caught in the sunlight,
she sways
weakened by her own grace.

Her fading hues
drain into the ground
where her roots lie deep;
earth's embrace beckoning
to her wilted frame.

She bends to the soil's call.
Even before
the sun scorched her,
the earth had her.

Soft white hairs, now worn,
and leaves humbly resigned;
she knows what nature will reclaim
and surrenders
her seed to fertile loam and tender sprouts.
